Description:

Pizza is a popular Italian dish made up of a round, flat dough base that's typically topped with tomato sauce, cheese (usually mozzarella), and various ingredients like meats, vegetables, and seasonings. It's baked in an oven until the crust is crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly. While the classic pizza is often called Margherita, which consists of just tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil, there are countless variations worldwide. Common toppings include pepperoni, mushrooms, onions, olives, bell peppers, and sausage. Some regions even have unique styles of pizza—like Chicago deep-dish or New York thin-crust.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 minced garlic clove (optional)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Pinch of sugar (optional)
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Salt and black pepper (to taste)

Steps:

  1. Prepare Dough
    • Mix flour, yeast, salt, water, and olive oil.
    • Knead and let it rise for 1-2 hours.
  2. Preheat Oven
    • Heat to 475-500°F (245-260°C).
  3. Shape Dough
    • Roll or stretch dough into a circle.
  4. Add Sauce
    • Spread tomato sauce evenly.
  5. Add Toppings
    • Add cheese, meats, veggies, etc.
  6. Bake Pizza
    • Bake for 10-15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  7. Serve
    • Let it cool slightly, slice, and enjoy!
